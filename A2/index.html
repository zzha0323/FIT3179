The military expenditure (or military budget) usually reflects how strongly a country perceives the likelihood of a threat to it or wishes to launch an invasion. The size of the budget also reflects a country's ability to fund military activities. Factors include the size of the country's economy, the entity's other financial needs, and the willingness of the entity's government or people to finance such military activities.

Military expenditure can be divided into two main categories: military expenditure by regions and military expenditure by countries.

This map visualisation shows the global military expenditure, focusing on understanding trends and variations in military spending across countries and regions over time.

The United States has historically had the most expensive military budget (1960 - 2022). Other countries like China, Russia, and India have significant increase in military expenditure through pass two decades.

Overall, the map shows that North America, East Asia and Western Europe are currently leading the way in terms of military expenditure. Keep in mind that large increases in military budgets can be caused by population growth, economic growth, and many other factors.

Military Expenditure by Regions

Military expenditure by regions refers to the amount of money that countries in specific geographic regions or continents allocate for their defense and military-related activities. This information is crucial for assessing regional security dynamics and understanding the distribution of defense resources globally.

The filled bubbles show the amount of military spending by continent in 2022.

North America has the highest military spending, with a budgeted cost of about $900 billion for 2022 alone, most of which is spent by the United States.

The area chart shows the total military expenditures of the five major regions over the past 30 years (1992-2022).

There is an overall increasing trend in all the regions.

Military Expenditure by Countries

Military Expenditure by Countries refers to the financial resources allocated by individual nations to support their defense and military operations. This data is used to analyse a nation's military capabilities, assess its defense priorities, and compare military spending between countries, which can have significant geopolitical implications.

The tree map shows the total military expenditures in 2022, including the top 20 countries in terms of military expenditures, which are grouped into different regions: Americas, Asia & Oceania, Europe and Middle East. The total military expenditures of other countries are also included in the group Other.

The United States has the most expensive military expenditure with an approximate total of 877 billion US dollars cost in 2022. Among the countries with the world's largest military budgets, China, India, France, Germany, Japan, Russia, the United Kingdom and the United States are often recognized as major powers.


The bar chart shows the eight countries with the highest military spending in 2022, comparing the past 30 years. The United States has historically had the most expensive military budget. China's military spending has increased dramatically over the past decade. Other countries' military spending has been nearly flat over this period. Notice that the military expenditure of Russia's military spending in 1992 is zero since the collapse of the Soviet Union.

The pie chart shows ratio of military expenditure of the top 8 countries in 2022, focusing on the three main groups: the United States, China and the remaining countries, or the Other.

The military expenditure of the top 8 countries in 2022 is also dominated by the United States, with the total military spending of around 876.94 billion US dollars that is greater than the sum of all other seven countries. On the other hand, China is catching up with the Other countries due to its domestic policies that kept its defense budget relatively stagnant until the 2000s.
